ABOUT ME - UNDER CONSTRUCTION


Hello there! I'm Thomas!

I'm a PhD student in Compuational Astrophysics at Cardiff University. My PhD focusses on cosmological simulations of massive galaxies, with specific focus on the Circumgalactic Medium (CGM) - the hot, thin, multiphase reservoir of gas that populates a galaxy's dark matter halo. The first part of my PhD focussed on the role of magnetic fields in ram pressure stripping of satellite galaxies in the CGM. I am currently investigating the role of anisotropic thermal conduction in the CGM of massive galaxies. My PhD work forms part of the SURGE (Simulating the Universe in Refined Galactic Environments) project.

I am also a member of the KILOGAS collaboration - an ALMA program observing the cold molecular gas of low-redshift galaxies - where I am a member of the theory working group. My work with KILOGAS seeks to use cosmological simulations to understand the origin of observed gas kinematic asymmetries.
