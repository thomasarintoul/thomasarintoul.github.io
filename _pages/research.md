My research lies in the realm of theoretical, computational astrophysics, using large-scale magnetohydrodynamics numerical simulations to explore the evolution of massive galaxies, and particularly their circumgalactic medium (CGM), in a cosmological context (rather than a "galaxy in a box", there is more of the universe around my galaxies). My research efforts are focussed currently on the effects of magnetic fields on the stripping of gas from satellite galaxies, and the effects of thermal conduction on the structure of the CGM.

I am supervised by [Dr Freeke van de Voort](https://profiles.cardiff.ac.uk/staff/vandevoortf) (primary) and [Dr Mattia Negrello](https://profiles.cardiff.ac.uk/staff/negrellom) (secondary).

Prior to my PhD, my Masters research project concerned star formation - specifically, using the smoothed particle hydrodynamics code, PHANTOM, to model the accretion of gas onto hierarchical triple star systems - long term stable systems of 3 stars. For this work, I was supervised by Prof. Ian Bonnell and Dr James Wurster, both of the University of St Andrews.
